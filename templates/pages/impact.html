The E-Waste Crisis Is Real.

Electronic waste is the world's fastest-growing solid waste stream. Every year, 62 million tonnes are generated globally — and less than 20% is formally recycled. eRecyclo is changing that, starting with India.

What Happens to Improperly Discarded Electronics

water_drop

Soil & Water Contamination

Lead, mercury, and cadmium from circuit boards and batteries leach into groundwater, contaminating drinking water sources that communities depend on for generations.

air

Toxic Air Emissions

Informal recyclers burn e-waste to recover copper and metals, releasing dioxins, furans, and heavy metal particulates into the air — causing respiratory damage in surrounding communities.

recycling

Wasted Precious Materials

Every tonne of discarded smartphones contains up to 300g of gold, 3kg of silver, and significant amounts of copper and palladium — rare metals lost forever in landfills.

check_circle The Solution

What Certified Recycling Achieves

spa

Safe Material Recovery

Certified recyclers extract gold, silver, copper, palladium, and rare-earth metals safely — returning them to the manufacturing supply chain without environmental harm.

co2

Carbon Reduction

Recycling metals from e-waste uses 95% less energy than primary mining. Every device properly recycled directly reduces CO₂ emissions and the demand for destructive mining operations.

water

Groundwater Protection

Proper disposal neutralizes toxic materials before they can enter the environment — protecting the water sources that villages, cities, and agricultural systems depend on.
